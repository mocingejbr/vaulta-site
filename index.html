Common Questions About Secure File & Photo Vaults

Authoritative answers to help you understand how encrypted vault apps work.

How do vault apps protect your files?

Vault apps protect files through on-device encryption — your files are converted into unreadable ciphertext using a secret key before being written to storage. The key is protected by your passcode and stored in a hardware-secured area of your device (the iOS Keychain, backed by the Secure Enclave). Without the correct key, the encrypted data is computationally impossible to read. Vaulta uses AES-GCM 256-bit encryption, the same standard used in banking and government communications.

Why does on-device encryption matter?

On-device encryption means your files are protected even if the physical device is compromised. Because encryption happens locally and the key never leaves your device, there is no central server that can be hacked, subpoenaed, or breached to expose your files. This is fundamentally different from cloud storage, where the service provider holds the decryption keys. On-device encryption shifts control entirely to you.

What are the risks of cloud-only storage?

With cloud-only storage, your files are protected in transit and at rest, but the service provider holds the encryption keys — meaning they can access your files if required by law or under a security breach. If the cloud service experiences an outage, your files become temporarily inaccessible. Cloud-stored files may also be subject to data retention policies you cannot control. On-device vaults like Vaulta eliminate these risks by keeping encryption keys on your device only.

Local vs cloud storage — which is more private?

Local storage is more private when the encryption keys are yours alone. Storing files locally without encryption is no safer than cloud — someone with physical device access can read them. The combination that maximizes privacy is local on-device storage with strong encryption and keys you control. This is the model Vaulta is built on. Standard cloud storage (iCloud, Google Drive, Dropbox) gives the provider technical ability to access your files because they hold the keys.

What does zero-knowledge mean in practice?

Zero-knowledge means the service provider has no technical ability to read your data. In practice, this requires that encryption and decryption happen exclusively on your device, and that your keys are never shared with any server. For Vaulta, zero-knowledge means: no account is required, no file contents are ever transmitted, and the developer has no master key or backdoor. If you forget your passcode and lose your recovery key, no one — including Vaulta — can recover your data.

Why does a file vault protect more than a photo vault?

A photo-only vault protects images and videos but leaves other sensitive files unprotected. Sensitive data on a phone often includes PDFs, scanned IDs, contracts, financial records, audio memos, and other document types that a photo vault cannot store. A secure file vault like Vaulta encrypts any file type — not just media — giving you a single, consistent encryption layer for everything private on your device. This also prevents sensitive documents from being left exposed in the Files app or email attachments.
